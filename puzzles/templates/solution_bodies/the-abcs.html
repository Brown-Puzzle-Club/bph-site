SHARKS Dividing the grid into these categories will have a few ambiguities, which can be resolved by understanding that each column (A-F) has one element of each category, with one exception. The correctly matched elements are:

NOVELS AMERICAN GODS BRAVE NEW WORLD THE CALL OF THE WILD THE DEVIL AND DANIEL WEBSTER EMMA FEAR AND LOATHING IN LAS VEGAS
SHARKS ANGEL BULL COOKIE CUTTER DALL'S PORPOISE EPAULETTE FINETOOTH
Each category also has exactly one “mistake”: an item that's similar but not in the actual category.
LEMURS EASTERN GREY Eastern Grey is a kind of squirrel, not lemur
QUEENS JOHN DEACON John Deacon is a bass guitarist for the British band Queen, not an actual regal queen
CITIES ANDREWS, OREGON Andrews, Oregon is a ghost town, not a city
APPLES D'ANJOU D'Anjou is a pear species, not an apple species
MOVIES DOCTOR WHO Doctor Who is primarily a TV show
PONIES CASS OLE Cass Ole is a real equine, not a pony from MLP
NOVELS THE DEVIL AND DANIEL WEBSTER The Devil and Daniel Webster is a short story
SHARKS DALL'S PORPOISE Dall's Porpoise is (you guessed it) a porpoise
We might now notice that each category is exactly 6 letters long. And there are 6 possible items (A-F) out of which one is a mistake. Using this, we can extract a letter! For example, MOVIES had the mistake as the 4th entry (Column D = Doctor Who) so we take the 4th letter of MOVIES.
A B C D E F
LEMURS EASTERN GREY L E M U R S
QUEENS JOHN DEACON Q U E E N S
CITIES ANDREWS, OREGON C I T I E S
APPLES D'ANJOU A P P L E S
MOVIES DOCTOR WHO M O V I E S
PONIES CASS OLE P O N I E S
NOVELS THE DEVIL AND DANIEL WEBSTER N O V E L S
SHARKS DALL'S PORPOISE S H A R K S
From top to bottom, this spells out our answer, RECLINER. {% endblock %} {% block author-notes-html %} Zach was originally the one to pitch an idea for this puzzle to me (although utilizing a slightly different flavor).
