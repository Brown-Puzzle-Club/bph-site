Knowing that locations correlate to elements, the map can be re-understood as the periodic table (when tilted 90 degrees). With this, each rectangle/enclosed space in the map becomes an element on the Manhattan Periodic Table. There are streets highlighted on the map that extend past enclosed portions, but two/three-sided regions that are not enclosed are not included as representing elements. Mapping the locations from the “Things to do section” generates the following elements:

The elements from the locations serve as the letters involved in the final answer: simply ordering them as they originally were results in N - As - I - Db - C - La. To obtain the answer, the resulting elements are ordered according to the subway line associated with each stop, provided in the “things to do section”. The subway lines do not correspond to the location of each spot (i.e. the 7 train does not service anywhere near the Nuyorican Poets Cafe), so teams could get hung up connecting them without any results. The correct ordering is drawn from using the ROYBGIV color sequence to reorder the location’s elements: Db (Red 1) - As (Orange D) - I (Yellow N) - C (Green 4) - La (Blue A) - N (Purple 7). This results in DBASICLAN, which when read cyclically gives the apt answer BASIC LAND. {% endblock %}
