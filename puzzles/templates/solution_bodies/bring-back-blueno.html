Which gives us our answer, EVIL PAWERS. {% endblock %} {% block author-notes-html %} Puzzle constructors who try to be clever should be run out into the streets. Here I am, writing a metapuzzle for an opening round for a hunt, that needs to strike a balance between easy (for new, on-site solvers) and accessible (for remote solvers who couldn't go around poking their fingers through statues). This meta proved to be neither, benefitting only the already-experienced teams who had people on-site.

Ah, who am I kidding. I still like this meta. I think it's cute, and it works diegetically within the narrative of the hunt (how often does that happen?), and drawing the pentagram is fun. But it should have cut back on everything else. Lessons for next time.

As a little bit of trivia, an original version of this puzzle (which was cut weeks before the hunt for not being remote-accessible enough - yes, really) was reshaped into what eventually became the final runaround. {% endblock %}
